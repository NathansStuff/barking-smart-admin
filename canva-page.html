EASY

SCENT

10Min

Puzzle Treat Box
Adventure

Materials Needed:

  • A small cardboard box (like a shoebox) or a basket
  • 3–5 smaller containers or items that can hold treats, such as:
  • Toilet paper rolls (fold the ends to create a treat tube)
  • Small Tupperware containers with lids (with a hole for sniffing)
  • Socks or small cloth pouches
  • High-value treats (like small bits of dog kibble or favorite treats

Setup

  1. Gather your materials and place them on the floor in your designated activity space.
  2. In each of the smaller containers, hide a small treat. Make sure some containers are easier to open
    than others, to create different levels of challenge.
  3. Place the smaller containers inside the cardboard box or basket. You can cover the main box with
    a cloth or leave it open, depending on your dog's comfort with different levels of challenge.
  1. Introduce the Game: Show your dog the treats before placing them in the containers to
    get them excited. Let them sniff around the box so they know there’s something worth
    working for.
  2. Encourage Problem Solving: Place the box on the floor and encourage your dog to
    explore it. Cheer them on as they sniff, paw, or nudge to get to the treats.
  3. Provide Hints (if needed): If your dog seems stuck, you can give them a little hint by
    opening one container slightly or guiding them with your hand. Keep encouraging
    them so they feel successful as they work through the puzzle.
  4. Celebrate Wins: Each time your dog solves a part of the puzzle, give them gentle praise
    or a small reward to keep them engaged and motivated.
  • Repeat and Rotate: You can repeat this activity a few times, swapping
    out different types of containers to keep it fresh.
  • Increase Challenge Over Time: Once your dog masters the basics, you
    can make it a bit harder by adding more containers or by partially
    hiding the main box under a light cover.
